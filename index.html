    • Programme Structure

    The program includes core papers in programming, databases, and networks, as well as electives in areas like cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and software development.
    To complete the degree you must follow one of the following pathways:

    Pathways

    • Single Major + Single Minor + Elective. For example Major in Computer Science, minor in Data Science, and elective courses
    • Single Major + First Minor + Second Minor. For example Major in Software Development, minor in Data Science, and minor in Digital Services
    • Double Major + Major. For example Major in Software Development and Major in Data Science

    * Electives can be any level 5 to level 7 paper

    The Minors offered from BCIS are AI, Data Science, Digital Services, Networks and Cybersecurtiy, Software Development, and Computer Science.

    Other Minors can be found at Additional Minors

    How to Select Courses?

    To complete the degree within 3 years you need to ensure you are enrolled in 4 courses each semester. In general you are allowed to select any course during any year in the degree as long as you met the pre-requisites. A recommended method to select your courses are to do it in the following order:

    1. What courses in my ‘core’ do I still need to complete
    2. What courses in my ‘major’ do I still need to complete
    3. What courses in my ‘minor’ do I still need to complete
    4. Do I need to do any electives.

    It is recommended for you to choose your courses in that order because of how flexible the requirements are. For example, ‘major’ courses only have 1 to 2 choices of which course to study whereas ‘minors’ and ‘electives’ have more. Therefore, ‘majors’ are more difficult to fit your schedule around so choose them first!
